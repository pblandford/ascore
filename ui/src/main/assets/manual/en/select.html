Selection and Clipboard

Long pressing a segment of the score brings up selection mode. While the segment is still pressed, a circle appears highlighting it - moving up and down will shift the notes in this segment up and down

Once the finger is released, the segment is highlighted in grey and the clipboard panel appears:

If the selection didn't quite appear where you wanted, use the left and right arrows to move it along:

The end of a selection can be chosen by pressing once at the required location:

The left and right arrows will now move the end of the selection back and forth.

To clear a selection, just tap in an empty part of the score. Alternately use the 'clear' button on the bottom row:

Press the 'copy' button to copy the contents of the selection to the clipboard:

To paste it somewhere else, long-press a segment in the score to make a new selection, and press the 'paste' button:

Using the 'cut' button instead of 'copy' will remove the original selection once it is pasted:

In addition, another panel appears at the bottom of the screen, allowing further editing of the selection

The up/down arrows on this panel allow you to move notes in the selection up or down:

Long pressing on these arrows will move them note up or down a whole octave.

Ties can be added across a selection with the tie button - any consecutive chords with notes in common will be tied:

The note head type of a selection can be changed with the selector shown:

The note heads in a selection can be rendered small with the button shown:

The stem button allows you to specify the direction of the stems of the notes in the selection - press it to toggle an up/down direction. To remove your settings and restore the defaults, long press the button

Individual notes in a chord can be selected by pressing the cycle button, allowing them to be moved, changed or deleted without affecting the rest of the chord
